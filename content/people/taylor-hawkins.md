Oliver Taylor Hawkins (February 17, 1972 - March 25, 2022) was an American musician best known as the drummer of the Foo Fighters from 1997 until his death in 2022. Hawkins joined the band after the departure of William Goldsmith during the recording of *The Colour and the Shape* and became a beloved figure in rock music, known for his energetic performances and warm personality.
