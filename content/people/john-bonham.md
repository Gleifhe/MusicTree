John Henry Bonham (May 31, 1948 - September 25, 1980) was a British drummer and one of rock's most celebrated percussionists. His thunderous, powerful style on albums like *Led Zeppelin IV* (1971) remains the benchmark for hard rock drumming. His death led to the dissolution of Led Zeppelin.
