Christina Maria Aguilera (born December 18, 1980) is an American singer, songwriter, and actress. Known for her powerful vocals, she rose to fame in 1999 with her self-titled debut album and won five Grammy Awards. Her 2002 album *Stripped*, produced largely by Linda Perry, marked a major artistic shift toward a rawer, more personal sound.
